# UNIPD Positive Energy District (PED) Strategic Action Plan

A multidisciplinary engineering framework for achieving Positive Energy District (PED) status in high-density historic urban environments. This project integrates TABULA-based thermal modeling, €134.2M CAPEX financial structuring, and heritage-compliant solar PV integration for the University of Padua.

## Key Technical Contributions
* [cite_start]**Building Clustering Strategy**: Segmented 26 buildings into **Modern (1970s-90s)** and **Historic (Pre-1945)** clusters to navigate strict Italian heritage preservation laws[cite: 194, 195, 197].
* [cite_start]**Energy Modeling**: Utilized the TABULA WebTool (IT.MidClim.AB.06.Gen archetype) to simulate a **38% primary energy reduction** through deep renovation of modern facilities[cite: 213, 214, 217].
* [cite_start]**Solar PV Integration**: Calculated a total annual solar generation potential of **3,942.42 MWh/year** across 14,415 m² of usable rooftop area[cite: 283, 284].
* [cite_start]**KPI Development**: Established Key Performance Indicators including Renewable Energy Share (RES) and Self-Consumption Rate (SCR)[cite: 368, 375].

## Technical Calculations
**Renovation Potential:**
$$Saving(\%) = \frac{EP_{existing} - EP_{refurbished}}{EP_{existing}} \times 100 \approx 38\%$$

**Solar Energy Generation:**
$$E_{solar} = \frac{P_{sys} \times Y_{solar}}{1000}$$
